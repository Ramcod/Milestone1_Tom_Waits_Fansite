Small Change

This masterpiece from 1976 is considered a real fan favourite. It has the feel of a 40's detective film noir, but set in the gutter. This is downtrodden Tom, working shifts so that he might be able to afford a whiskey, and perhaps a night out with his lady. Top tracks include 'Step Right Up', 'Tom Traubert's Blues' and 'The Piano Has Been Drinking (Not Me)'.

Rain Dogs

Released in 1985 this is part of a 3 album release, that saw Tom reach cult status. The sounds on this are quite unique and sees Tom combine polka, with New Orleans brass bands, and good old rock 'n' roll working alongside Keith Richards. It also sees Tom experiment with industrial sounds and some of the album highlights include, 'Big Black Mariah', 'Time', and 'Gun Street Girl'.

Big Time

A real hit on the senses with this visual 'live' recording. Tom is fine form, with his live band, and this is no ordinary concert movie. Only ever released on VHS, copies are as rare as hen's teeth but you can catch excerpts on the 'Tom Live' page.The first time I saw this I wasn't sure if I was half asleep, as was like watching the weirdest dream you've had but with incredible music.

Mule Variations

Perhaps Tom's finest work and the number 1 choice of many fans. Released in 1999 and with some old collaborators including Smokey Hormel and Marc Ribot, there is a real diversity to the album. Highlights include the beautiful 'House Where Nobody Lives' and the eccentric 'Chocolate Jesus'.

Bad As Me

After a break of around 7 years, this album shows Tom's full palette and has a list of some of Tom's favourite collaboraters, including Keith Richards. This has been the last studio album and Tom has been focussing on acting roles including the brilliant 'Ballad of Buster Scruggs'

Bone Machine

Released in 1992, this album features one of Tom's biggest commercial hits with 'I Don't Wanna Grow Up'.This was about the time that I tried to introduce Tom to my friends, and I'm still trying 30 years later. There are lots of albums to choose from with a style for most people, so have a listen.
